Homi Jehangir Bhabha

Father of Indian Nuclear Programme

Biography

Homi Jehangir Bhabha, born on October 30, 1909, in Mumbai, India, was a renowned nuclear physicist and a key figure in India's scientific history. He made significant contributions to the field of nuclear physics and played a pivotal role in the development of India's nuclear program.

Bhabha's passion for science led him to pursue his education at the University of Cambridge, where he achieved remarkable success in his academic career. His work in cosmic ray research earned him recognition in the scientific community.

In 1945, Bhabha founded the Tata Institute of Fundamental Research (TIFR) in Mumbai, which became a prominent center for research in nuclear physics and other fundamental sciences.

Awards and Achievements

  • Padma Bhushan: Homi Jehangir Bhabha was honored with the Padma Bhushan in 1954 for his outstanding contributions to science and his leadership in India's nuclear program.

Tragic Death

In a tragic turn of events, Homi Bhabha's life was cut short on January 24, 1966, when Air India Flight 101 crashed near Mont Blanc, France. He was en route to Vienna for a meeting related to his work in nuclear energy and research. The scientific community and India as a whole mourned the loss of this visionary scientist.
